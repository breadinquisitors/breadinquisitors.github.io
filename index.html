Gender Wage Gap

Overall Gender Wage Differences (1990 - 2019)

Presented below is an overall view of the gender wage differences between men and women, from 1990 to 2019. This view considers all racial, ethnic, religious, and age groups in the United States, with men being in blue and women being in pink.


Source: US Department of Labor

Of course, wages can vary by state, as positions, laws, societal norms, and/or population all differ from state to state. Thus, a United States map featuring all 50 state wage gaps is presented below. When highlighting each state, the name of the state, the median weekly earning difference percentage, and the respective weekly median earnings per gender group are presented for that corresponding state. The range in percentage difference is 3.04% to 31.65%.

Racial Gender Wage Differences (1990 - 2019)

Here, in order to allow for easier intuition, four charts were made to separate racial groups and view gender gaps between them. This is the same data presented as the overall gender wage gap, but instead split by racial category median, also within the 1990-2019 time frame. Likewise, separating these four racial groups allows for identifying how different racial groups stack up against one another, versus just the overall gender pay gap.


Source: US Department of Labor
